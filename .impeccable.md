## Design Context

### Users
Developers and hiring managers evaluating a cost accounting portfolio prototype. They view this in a browser during a code/design review, scanning quickly to assess both technical competence and design sensibility. They are impressed by specificity — a design that clearly understands the financial domain.

### Brand Personality
Precise, institutional, unsparing. The interface should feel like a serious financial instrument — not a SaaS dashboard, not a generic admin panel. Every element earns its place.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light mode. Cool blue-tinted off-white background (`oklch(96.5% 0.005 255)`). Deep petrol accent (`oklch(38% 0.13 208)`) — distinctive, avoids semantic conflict with green/red financial data.

Typography: Barlow SemiCondensed (display/headings) + Barlow (body). Geometric, slightly condensed, institutional. Not in the reflex reject list.

References: Bloomberg data density, FT.com typographic restraint, institutional financial tools.
Anti-references: Generic SaaS dashboards, cards-within-cards, glassmorphism, gradient text, border-left accent stripes.

### Design Principles
1. **Density over decoration** — tabular-nums, compact rows, uppercase tracking labels. Data should feel queryable, not decorative.
2. **Typography does the heavy lifting** — strong hierarchy through size and weight, not color and shadows.
3. **Semantic color is reserved** — green/red carry financial meaning (positive/negative); the accent color must not conflict.
4. **Tables are the primary UI** — financial tools live in tables. Treat them as first-class design surfaces.
5. **Every word earns its place** — labels are short and precise. No redundant column headers or repeated information.
